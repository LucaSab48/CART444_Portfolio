Artist Biography

Technology operates here as both material and conceptual framework.

Luca Sabelli is a Montreal-based artist working across the mediums of video, game development, and interactive media. He is currently completing a Bachelor of Fine Arts with a specialization in Computational Arts at Concordia University. His practice examines themes of identity and the surreal, using digital technology as a conceptual framework.

The digital is not only a vessel for the work, but also a way to explore how technology influences perception and shapes the ways presence and meaning are constructed. Through audiovisuals and interactive systems, Sabelli creates worlds that investigate how computational environments mediate experiences of reality.

Using game engines, generative systems, and moving images, he creates pieces that study the relationship between lived experience and virtual space. His work often focuses on technological systems and the moments where perception becomes ambiguous, revealing how agency and meaning are produced through partial information.

Influenced by experimental film and interactive digital art, Sabelli creates works that reflect on how technological systems shape perception and structure experience, building visual, spatial, and interactive worlds where the artificial can feel strangely familiar.
